Silence

Flatanger Cave, Norway
5.15d

Description

The world's first 5.15d, featuring a knee-bar rest in complete inversion and a near-impossible crux sequence. First climbed by Adam Ondra in 2017 after extensive projecting, this route represents one of climbing's greatest achievements, combining power, technical precision, and innovative beta.

The route follows an intricate line through the massive overhanging cave, with multiple crux sequences separated by marginal rests. The defining sequence involves a completely inverted position with a figure-four move between minuscule holds, requiring extraordinary body tension and core strength.

Beta & Features

  • Begins with powerful moves on small crimps leading to the first crux
  • Mid-route contains the famous upside-down knee-bar rest, requiring extreme flexibility
  • The primary crux features a figure-four move between shallow pockets
  • Final section requires powerful compression on slopers with heel hooks
  • Total route length is approximately 45 meters with sustained difficulty
